Hi! I am currently a Senior Computer Engineering undergraduate at Texas A&M University in College Station. Some of my hobbies include cooking, home automation, and cybersecurity.

I recently completed a IT internship at Phillips 66, one of the most popular downstream Oil Companies in the global industry. During my time there, I conducted a Proof of Concept evaluation of some exciting new technology - AWS Monitron - to vet whether or not it might add value to the Lubricants business, and potentially the Refining business. I met regularly with on-site personel, department leads, and team members of the Amazon Monitron team. Additionally, I automated a few workflows to save time and provide insight into lubricants sales, minimizing profit loss. My experience at Phillips not only taught me about the business, but how it was run. I got to meet with C-level executives such as Zhanna Golodryga - Chief Digital & Administrative Officer. Such opportunities are what make me so incredibly grateful for this experience.

Before then, I had the fortunate chance to participate in Texas A&M's Aggie Research Program, where I worked with a team of 6 people to processing .fits images from NASA's Hubble and Swift Satellites. Each image captured by a satellite would have a good deal of 'noise', or what appeared as a dusty cloud stretching across the image, making it hazy and unclear. Our task was to 'clean' the image of this noise without degrading image quality, keeping stars and galaxies resolute and intact. To do this we wrote a series of python scripts utilizing a number of libraries, namely astropy. After the project was complete, we presented our results to a panel of judges, composed of faculty and former NASA employees, and won the award Aggie Research Scholar.

I also enjoy a number of hobbies, namely hydroponics, cooking, and recurve archery. My interest in hydroponics came about when I started cooking neopolitan style pizzas from scratch.
