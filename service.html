  • International Student Association

    My life in middle and high school revolved around competing in Speech and Debate. I was enchanted with the process of speaking in front of crowds, preparing impromptu speeches, and debating against like-minded opponents. Once my time was up, I wanted to give back and teach the skills that I had learned from my time competing. During my summers, I coached middle school students on how to better engage in their speaking events, giving advice on how best to approach different speaking situations. I also personally counseled students who needed some extra support and motivation to speak in front of larger crowds.

  • Local tutor at community Mosque

    • Whenever possible, I try to help members of my community in their schoolwork. Recently, due to COVID, many of my peers have found it hard to keep up in their STEM classes, citing that the material is often too hard to grasp when working alone in their houses. I sympathize with this as I often had trouble in these classes as well during middle and high school. Once a week, I host a group Zoom meeting in which members of my local mosque can ask me questions about material that they are learning in class that they find challenging.

  • Open Source contributor

    Reading The Cathedral & the Bazaar gave me a newfound respect for the open-source community. Now that I feel confident in myself as a programmer, I believe it is my responsibility to give back to the community that helped me learn so much. In my free time, I aim to start contributing to various open-source projects listed here that may need some extra helping hands to keep the ball rolling.
