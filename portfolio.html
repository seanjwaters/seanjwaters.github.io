  • Ethereum GPU Mining Rig

    I used my entire internship savings to invest in the hardware and software needed to build an ethereum mining rig. It uses a custom unix-based OS and required extensive configuration of the physical system to enable the use of 8 graphics cards. One key concern was safety, and ensuring the overall power draw was safe.

  • NASA Hubble & Swift Satellite De-Noiser

    My freshman year, I found myself on a small team helping Dr. Peter Brown filter throuhg .fits image data. My team and I were tasked with automating a portion of his workflow, specifically the part where he manually goes through each image to filter out the "background noise" that gets captured in satellite images, which appears as a dusty cloud, stretching over the whole picture. While basic graphics filters would make the image clearer, it would also degrade the quality. Using the python astropy library among others, we were able to remove the noise and retain image quality. Some of our results can be seen here.

  • Custom Keyboard

    Building a custom keyboard is another way I gained experience with pcbs and soldering next to my Proxmark 3 & Boosted Board. To build the custom wooden keyboard case I designed the model in Rhino 3D, generating the gcode in DeskProto, and routing the design on a full size CNC router. It was particularly tricky implementing a 5 degree angle.

  • Synchronized Sensors for Vehicle Autopilot

    This was my Senior Capstone project, where my team and I synchronized a Lidar and Camera sensor to the PPS signal of a Reach M2 GPS. To do this, we used the RIAPs platform, hosted on a BeagleBone Black, to process the GPS PPS signal and output that to our Lidar and Camera sensors, synchronizing their output frequency for the 'Autodrive' system.
